Positive Selection Signals Database (PSSDB)

Harmonized signals of recent positive selection across multiple species, aggregated from peer-reviewed studies. Browse by species, chromosome, genomic range, or gene; results include normalized coordinates, key metrics (Fst, XP-EHH, iHS, etc.), assembly context, and full provenance.

How to use

Filters are orthogonal. Use any subset of filters; anything you leave blank defaults to “all”. Selected filters are intersected.

1) Explore a species

Want the full picture for one species? Choose the species and run the search (you can leave chromosome, range, and gene empty). You’ll get: all curated signals for that species — genes, SNPs and window-based hits merged from multiple studies — with normalized coordinates, key metrics (Fst, XP-EHH, iHS, etc.), assembly, populations (when applicable), and provenance. Use the visual panel below to spot notable regions at a glance.

2) Focus on a gene

Interested in a particular gene’s evolutionary story? Select the gene (optionally pick a species). You’ll get: all selection signals overlapping that gene (and nearby SNPs/windows) in the chosen species. If you run this for different species, you can quickly see where the gene shows evidence of recent positive selection.

3) Investigate a genomic region

Curious about a specific locus? Pick a species, then set a chromosome and either a start–end range or leave the range empty to scan the whole chromosome. You’ll get: every signal that falls inside the region — ideal for checking whether a candidate interval carries evidence of selection. Notes Coordinates are in base pairs, 1-based inclusive. Leaving a field empty broadens scope (e.g., “species only” lists everything for that species). Results always show the source study so you can trace every record back to the paper/supplement.

Contribute & keep PSSDB growing

PSSDB is community-driven and updated continuously. If you know a study that reports recent positive-selection signals, please submit it — the pipeline will handle the rest. What to upload The article PDF. All supplementary files from the paper (tables/appendices) in their original formats (XLSX/CSV/TSV/DOCX/PDF/TXT). What happens next The ingestion pipeline extracts, normalizes, annotates, validates, and adds the signals to the database. Records are linked to the paper’s DOI/PMID and include full provenance. (Optional but helpful) Include the species and genome assembly if stated in the paper. Your submissions help keep the database
